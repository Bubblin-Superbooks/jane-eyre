“How can she bear it so quietly—so firmly?” I asked of myself.  “Were I in her place, it seems to me I should wish the earth to open and swallow me up.  She looks as if she were thinking of something beyond her punishment—beyond her situation: of something not round her nor before her.  I have heard of day-dreams—is she in a day-dream now?  Her eyes are fixed on the floor, but I am sure they do not see it—her sight seems turned in, gone down into her heart: she is looking at what she can remember, I believe; not at what is really present.  I wonder what sort of a girl she is—whether good or naughty.”
